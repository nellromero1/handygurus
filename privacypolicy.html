What is personally identifiable information?

Personally identifiable information (PII) is any data that could potentially identify a specific individual. This includes information such as name, address, phone number, email address, Social Security number, driver’s license number, passport number, financial account numbers, and biometric data such as fingerprints or facial recognition data. Essentially, any information that could be used to identify an individual, either on its own or in combination with other data, is considered PII. PII is sensitive information that must be protected, as its unauthorized disclosure or use could lead to identity theft, fraud, and other harms.
